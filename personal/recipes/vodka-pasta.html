Vodka Pasta

medium

Creamy, flavorful vodka sauce pasta with a hint of heat - a perfect weeknight dinner that tastes like it came from a restaurant.

Prep: 10 mins
Cook: 20 mins
Serves: 4

Ingredients

16 oz (2 cups) pasta
4 tbsp olive oil
4 small cloves (diced) garlic
2 (minced) shallots
1 tsp Italian seasoning
¼ cup tomato paste
½ cup heavy cream
1 tbsp vodka
1 tsp red pepper flakes
1 tbsp butter
¼ cup (grated) parmesan cheese
To taste (chopped) basil
To taste salt and pepper
1-2 tsp (optional) Calabrian chile paste

Instructions

  1. 1

    Begin by bringing a large pot of salted water to a boil for the pasta (16 oz (2 cups)).

  2. 2

    In a large skillet over medium heat

  3. 3

    melt butter (1 tbsp)

  4. 4

    then add olive oil (4 tbsp) and heat until it shimmers.

  5. 5

    Add minced garlic (4 small cloves (diced)) and shallots (2 (minced)) to the pan. Sauté until fragrant and translucent

  6. 6

    about 2-3 minutes.

  7. 7

    Stir in Italian seasoning (1 tsp) and cook for 30 seconds

  8. 8

    then add tomato paste (¼ cup). Cook

  9. 9

    stirring frequently

  10. 10

    for 2-3 minutes until the tomato paste (¼ cup) darkens slightly.

  11. 11

    Add vodka (1 tbsp) to the pan and let it simmer for about 1-2 minutes to cook off the alcohol.

  12. 12

    Lower the heat and slowly pour in heavy cream (½ cup)

  13. 13

    stirring constantly. If using

  14. 14

    add Calabrian chile paste (1-2 tsp (optional)) for extra heat and depth of flavor.

  15. 15

    Add red pepper flakes (1 tsp) and salt and pepper (To taste) to taste. Let the sauce simmer gently for 5 minutes

  16. 16

    stirring occasionally.

  17. 17

    Meanwhile

  18. 18

    cook pasta (16 oz (2 cups)) according to package directions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta (16 oz (2 cups)) water before draining.

  19. 19

    Add the drained pasta (16 oz (2 cups)) to the sauce

  20. 20

    along with 1/4 cup of the reserved pasta (16 oz (2 cups)) water. Toss to coat the pasta (16 oz (2 cups)) evenly.

  21. 21

    Stir in grated parmesan cheese (¼ cup (grated)) until melted and sauce is creamy. If sauce is too thick

  22. 22

    add more pasta (16 oz (2 cups)) water a little at a time.

  23. 23

    Serve hot

  24. 24

    garnished with freshly chopped basil (To taste (chopped)) and additional grated parmesan if desired.

Notes

The addition of Calabrian chile paste brings a complex, fruity heat that elevates this classic dish. It's optional but highly recommended if you enjoy a bit of spice. For best results, use a shaped pasta like rigatoni or penne that can catch the sauce in its ridges and hollows. This sauce can be made ahead and refrigerated for up to 3 days—just reheat gently and add a splash of pasta water when combining with freshly cooked pasta.
